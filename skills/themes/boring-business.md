## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Talking Down to the Audience
**Problem:** Sounds condescending ("They're not sophisticated enough for complex tools")
**Fix:** Frame as "They're smart enough to prioritize ROI over features"

### Mistake 2: Overcomplicating Solutions
**Problem:** Suggesting tools/approaches too complex for their needs
**Fix:** Default to simplest solution that works

### Mistake 3: Not Quantifying ROI
**Problem:** Vague claims like "improves efficiency"
**Fix:** Always include specific time/money saved

### Mistake 4: Positioning as Consultant
**Problem:** Violates employment safety + feels salesy
**Fix:** Frame as observation + helping friends

### Mistake 5: Ignoring Industry Nuances
**Problem:** Generic advice that doesn't fit their reality
**Fix:** Reference specific industry challenges (seasonality, truck logistics, etc.)
