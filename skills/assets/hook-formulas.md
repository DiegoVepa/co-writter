## Tier 1 Hooks (Use Most)

### 1. Contrarian Take

**Formula:**
"Most [people/companies] do X. [Unexpected alternative/truth]."

- Challenges conventional wisdom
- Creates tension
- Promises fresh perspective

### 3. Unexpected Reframe

**Formula:**
"Everyone's talking about [X]. The real [insight] is [Y]."
OR
"It's not about [obvious thing]. It's about [deeper insight]."

**Why It Works:**
- Acknowledges common view
- Offers deeper perspective
- Positions you as thoughtful

**Examples:**

```

```

```

```

```

```

```

It's that nobody thought through the workflow it's replacing.
```

```
Everyone's building AI features for customers.

The best ROI? Internal operations nobody sees.
```

**When to Use:**
- Adding nuance to trending topics
- Sharing counter-intuitive lessons
- Thought leadership positioning
- Systems thinking demonstrations

**Audience Fit:**
- Career Pivot: ‚úÖ‚úÖ Shows product/strategic thinking
- Agency Build: ‚úÖ Demonstrates depth

## Tier 3 Hooks (Use Sparingly)

### 7. Personal Story

**Formula:**
"[Time period] ago, I [did something]. [Result or reflection]."

**Why It Works:**
- Humanizes you
- Creates relatability
- Can be emotionally engaging

**Examples:**

```

Yesterday I shipped an automation that saves my team 25 hours/week.

Here's what I learned.
```

```
In 10 years of selling, I never thought I'd be building internal tools.

Now it's my favorite part of the job.
```

```
Last year I manually did something 52 times.

This year I automated it. Now it happens automatically every week.

Why did I wait so long?
```

**When to Use:**
- Authentic vulnerable moments
- Career transition reflections
- Major milestones
- When personal context adds value

**Caution:** Can feel self-indulgent if overused.

**Audience Fit:**
- Career Pivot: ‚úÖ Signals growth and adaptability

## Hook Formula Combinations

Sometimes the strongest hooks combine multiple formulas:

### Contrarian + What I Built:
```
Most sales teams automate the wrong things.

This week I built a workflow that automates the actual bottleneck:
getting product specs into quotes.

2 days ‚Üí 20 minutes.
```

### Problem-First + Before/After:
```
My friend was losing $2K/month to no-shows.

Before: 30% no-show rate.
After: 12%.

Cost to fix: $15/month.
```

### Counter-Intuitive + Reframe:
```
I used to think automation was about saving time.

It's not. It's about reducing friction.

The time savings are just a side effect.
```

## Hook Writing Process

### Step 1: Identify Core Message
What's the main point of this post?

### Step 2: Choose Hook Type
Which formula fits this message best?

### Step 3: Draft Hook
Write 2-3 variations using the formula.

### Step 4: Test for Scroll-Stop
Read each version and ask:
- Would I stop scrolling for this?
- Does it create curiosity?
- Is it authentic to my voice?

### Step 5: Refine
- Cut unnecessary words
- Strengthen specificity
- Ensure it flows into the post body

## Common Hook Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Too Vague
‚ùå "Here's something interesting about AI."
‚úÖ "Most AI pilots fail because of integration, not the AI."

### Mistake 2: Burying the Lead
‚ùå "I've been thinking a lot about automation lately and wanted to share some thoughts about what I've learned after working on a few projects..."
‚úÖ "Built a workflow this week that cut quote turnaround from 2 days to 20 minutes."

### Mistake 3: Clickbait Without Payoff
‚ùå "This one trick will change your business forever." (Then shares a generic tip)
‚úÖ "Built a 45-minute automation that saves $2K/month. Here's how." (Then delivers specifics)

### Mistake 4: Too Generic
‚ùå "AI is changing everything."
‚úÖ "Most companies don't need AI. They need to fix their broken processes first."

### Mistake 5: Humble-Bragging
‚ùå "Just closed the biggest deal of my career while also launching three new AI projects."
‚úÖ "Built three automations this month. Two work great. One completely failed. Here's what I learned."
