## LinkedIn Caption

After 10 years in B2B sales, these are the 5 things I wish had been automated from day one.

Lead follow-ups.
I can't count how many deals went cold because a follow-up slipped through the cracks on a busy week.

Lead qualification.
Hours spent on calls with prospects who were never going to buy. That time doesn't come back.

Pipeline updates.
Every Friday: "Can everyone update their CRM before the meeting?" Every Friday: half the team didn't.

Appointment scheduling.
The back-and-forth emails to find 30 minutes. Sometimes the scheduling took longer than the meeting.

Performance insights.
End of month scramble to pull numbers from three dashboards just to answer "how are we doing?"

None of these are hard problems.
But when they depend on someone remembering to do them, they break constantly.

Today, all five can be handled by simple systems that cost less than a team lunch.

The shift isn't about replacing people.
It's about freeing them to do the work that actually moves deals.

Which of these five is still manual on your team?
